The year was 1932, in pre-independent Kenya.
The school had a purely Asian population, a community that lives, to this day, around the Ngara, mwatate area in Taita Taveta. The school offered what was then the Cambridge and Higher Cambridge School Certificate; today’s equivalent of ‘O’ and ‘A’ level education. After Independence in 1963, the school was renamed;Mwatate High School and enrolment of African students steadily began. It was initially mixed day school, but was converted to a boarding school in 1986.
At the time it was then considered a provincial school.">
